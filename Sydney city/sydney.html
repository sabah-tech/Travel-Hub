Discover Sydney

Everyone recognises Sydney for the famous Sydney Opera House

Welcome to Sydney

Sydney, capital of New South Wales and one of Australia's largest cities, is best known for its harbourfront Sydney Opera House, with a distinctive sail-like design. Massive Darling Harbour and the smaller Circular Quay port are hubs of waterside life with the arched Harbour Bridge and esteemed Royal Botanic Garden nearby

Top Attraction Places in Sydney

Sydney Opera House

The Sydney Opera House is a multi-venue performing arts centre in Sydney, New South Wales, Australia. Located on the foreshore of Sydney Harbour.

Sydney Tower Eye

The Sydney Tower Eye has been an integral part of the famous Sydney skyline for the past 30 years. Located 250 metres above the Sydney streets.

St Mary's Cathedral

St Mary's Cathedral is the cathedral church of the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Sydney and the seat of the Archbishop of Sydney, currently Anthony Fisher OP.

Sydney Harbour Bridge

The Sydney Harbour Bridge is a steel through arch bridge in Sydney, New South Wales, Australia, spanning Sydney Harbour from the central business district

Food in Sydney

Try Barramundi

Barramundi s a quintessential Australian fish that’s served in restaurants all over the country but is particularly common in Sydney

Taste Yellowfin tuna

Yellowfin tuna s a common fish used in sashimi and sushi, but its abundant population in Australian waters makes this one of the most popular fish in Sydney

Explore fried potato cake

The fried potato cake is a common staple that’s loved by many Australians for its rich, crispy outer shell and soft, airy inside
